Lesson 5 - Hardware interrupts

A hardware interrupt will allow you to call a special function called an interrupt service routine (ISR) at any point while your program is running.
See this example for how one works.
Most examples use the digitalPinToInterrupt() function call as the first parameter of the attachInterupt() function but you should be able to use a good pinout diagram to determine what interrupt channel coresponds to what pin.
The second parameter in the attachInterrupt() function is a good name for your interrupt service routine; it should be obvious what your ISR is for and is doing by its name.
The third parameter determines when the interrupt will trigger using macros FALLING, HIGH, LOW, RISING, and BOTH. Not all macros are supported on all boards.
This all has to do with the voltage bevavior on the interrupt pin.
The one you may use the most is FALLING b/c that is what will happen to the voltage when you press a button that is connected to an internal pullup.
