Using the HuskyLens w/ robot state machine

Here are the resources you need to get started on this lesson: HuskyLens API | HuskyLens Wiki Also here is the Tinkercad activity on what a state machine can look like for your robot and why you need one.

A state machine is just a fancy way to say that you want your robot to be in a stable behavior based upon its inputs and what behavior it is currently in.
If you just relied on the inputs to change your robot's behavior without remembering its current state, then you would be constantly calling functions without need.
A typical state machine is just a series of if / else if / else statements. To make it simple, each condition should be mutually exclusive (i.e. unique).
The default condition (else) is optional. This could be a failsafe to throw an error that your inputs went out of range.

Spin and get ball challenge

Your husklens will be able to give you information on whether or not it sees the ball after you train it.
Constantly read in data from the husklens in your loop function and setup a simple state machine so that your robot's complex behavior is to spin until it sees the ball and then go forward when it sees the ball.
