How you approach a challenge is more relevant than solving it. These challenges have common solutions but only because people already applied the engineering design process to discover these solutions. We use challenges with common solutions to practice and hone our approach so that we may find solutions to challenges that are yet to be discovered. If you fail, you've simply found an approach that doesn't work which may make you better. Make a goal to learn what to focus on, what to test, and what you can ignore. For you to truly design, there cannot be any instructions! These challenges are deceptive in that they seem simple but do not get discouraged - simple tasks we do everyday like walking, riding a bike, playing a sport, or playing a music instrument all took many hours of practice - the same goes for learning how to engineer!
